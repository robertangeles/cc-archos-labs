> **Superseded 2026-05-07** by [[2026-05-07-linear-redesign]]. The four-section structure (hero → services → filter → closing CTA), the mailto CTA, the no-pricing decision, and most of the copy carry forward unchanged into the Linear-quality redesign. The section-level styling, typography, and layout are completely replaced.

Sendable home page at `archoslabs.xyz`. Four sections — hero, what we do, who we work with (filter), closing CTA. Single primary action: `mailto:rob.angeles@archoslabs.xyz` for "Book a call". Long-form editorial flow, no images, no animations, no third-party widgets.

## Decision

### Section structure

| Section | Container | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Hero | `max-w-3xl` | Claim + problem framing + positioning + primary CTA |
| What we do | `max-w-5xl`, 3-col grid | Three service lines, each with copy + price |
| Who we work with | `max-w-5xl`, 2-col grid | Built for / Not for — the explicit filter |
| Closing CTA | `max-w-3xl`, centered | Restate the offer in plain terms; second CTA |

Sections separated by `border-t border-rule` hairlines. Vertical padding scales with breakpoint (`py-20 md:py-28`).

### Hero copy
- **Headline:** "Most AI programs fail before the model arrives."
- **Sub 1:** "They fail because the data underneath them was never ready — ungoverned, unmodelled, and unfit for the decisions being made on top of it."
- **Sub 2:** "Archos Labs sits in the gap between the firms paid to tell you that, and the practitioners buried inside your competitors."

The headline replaces the Phase 0 placeholder ("We solve the problem that AI programs ignore"). The new line is a practitioner observation, not a marketing claim — exactly the voice CLAUDE.md mandates.

### Service-line cards
Three articles, each: serif `text-xl` heading, sans body. **No pricing.** Service names are: AI Readiness Assessment, Data Architecture, AI Agent Development.

### The filter
Two columns: "Built for" / "Not for". Three bullets each. Direct without naming dollar figures: the "Not for" list includes "Teams shopping for a partner logo over a senior practitioner" — Big Four positioning preserved, dollar amount removed. Per CLAUDE.md voice: "Direct. Honest. No corporate speak. No vanity metrics. No Big Four padding."

### Pricing — deliberately absent
**No dollar figures appear anywhere on the home page.** Reversed mid-Phase-0a per Rob's direction: "Remove the prices entirely from the home page. The CTA does the work instead. 'One call. Thirty minutes.' That's where price gets discussed — in a conversation, with context, with scope understood." This applies to both Archos's own rates ($1,100 day / $3,000 fixed) and Big Four reference rates ($2,500 day) — both flavours of pricing have been pulled.

The previous version of this page argued that visible pricing filters tyre-kickers. That reasoning was overruled: a senior practitioner discusses fee in conversation once scope is understood, not on a billboard. Pricing on the home page reads transactional; absence reads consultative. The CLAUDE.md project overview still documents the rates ($1,100 day / $3,000 fixed) — that's the canonical source for what to quote in the call. They just don't appear in marketing surface.

### Primary CTA
- Element: `<a>` with `mailto:rob.angeles@archoslabs.xyz?subject=Consulting%20enquiry`
- Style: solid `bg-accent` button, `text-paper`, inverts on hover (`hover:bg-paper hover:text-accent`)
- Repeated once in hero, once at page end. Both identical.

`mailto:` is intentional: the contact endpoint and form are Phase 1 items 10–11. A live mailto beats a `#contact` dead anchor and ships immediately.

### Hero font sizing
`text-4xl sm:text-5xl md:text-6xl` (36 / 48 / 60 px). The `text-4xl` mobile size replaces an earlier `text-5xl` because the longer headline wrapped to four short lines on a 390 px viewport — the worst being an orphaned "before the". Dropping to 36 px gives a clean three-line wrap: "Most AI programs / fail before the / model arrives."

A forced `<br className="hidden sm:inline">` between "fail" and "before" controls the desktop break; mobile uses natural wrap.

## Reasoning

The skeptical-CDO test ("would a CDO at a major Australian bank trust this enough to book a call") forced four constraints:

1. **Pricing absent from the home; price gets discussed in the call.** The home page sets the filter and demonstrates voice. Once a buyer self-qualifies and books, fee is discussed with scope understood. This matches how senior practitioners actually transact — and matches the CTA copy promise ("we tell you whether your problem is one we can solve — and what it would actually cost").
2. **Filter is on the home, not buried in About.** A self-respecting executive doesn't want to discover at minute 30 of a sales call that they're not the right fit. The filter on the home page does the disqualification immediately and signals that we know what we're not.
3. **One offer, one CTA, repeated.** No newsletter signup. No "learn more" maze. No diagnostic teaser yet. A single primary action — book a call — ships now and will be replaced with a form once `/api/contact` exists (Phase 1 items 10–11).
4. **Copy specificity over claims.** "BI dashboards from 2014", "twenty-five years deep", "partner logo over a senior practitioner" — concrete details beat adjectives. CLAUDE.md UI principle: "Specificity over generality."

## Verification

Verified 2026-05-07 via `node scripts/screenshot.mjs` at desktop (1280x800) and mobile (390x844).

- Desktop: hero headline 60 px, two-line break after "fail"; three-column service grid; two-column filter; both CTAs render as solid accent buttons.
- Mobile: hero headline 36 px, three-line natural wrap; service lines, filter columns, and CTA stack vertically; no horizontal overflow at 390 px.
- `pnpm tsc --noEmit` passes with no output.

Phase 0 item 3 verify criterion ("a skeptical first-time visitor can answer 'what is this and is it for me?' in under 10 seconds; page is sendable to a prospect"): met above the fold on desktop (claim + problem framing + CTA visible without scrolling); met within first scroll on mobile.

## What this does NOT decide

- The mailto destination address. `rob.angeles@archoslabs.xyz` is a sensible placeholder — Rob to confirm/replace before sending the URL to a prospect, since the inbox needs to actually exist.
- Routes for `/tools`, `/consulting`, `/modelling-room`. Header nav links them; pages are 404 until later phases.
- Contact form UX (Phase 1 items 10–11).
- SEO meta beyond the existing `metadata` in `app/layout.tsx`. Phase 1 item 12 owns title/description/OG.
- Privacy + terms (Phase 1 item 13).
